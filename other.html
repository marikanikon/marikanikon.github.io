Production and Organization

Aichi Prefectural Art Theater
2024-Present / Producer of Dance, Nagoya Japan
Aichi Prefectural Art Theater is a public theater in Nagoya, Japan, that consists of a Main Theater (2480 seats), Concert Hall (1800 seats), and a flexible Blackbox Theater (up to 330 seats). The there hosts programs all year long in the field of dance, music, and theater.

My role as producer includes:
  • Producing new works by emerging Japanese contemporary dance artists to be premiered at the Blackbox Theater.
  • Promoting and coordinating international tours of Japanese contemporary dance works.
  • Organizing and hosting multi-city Japan tours of international dance companies to be presented at the Main Theater.
  • Managing budget and applying for grants.
  • Building international networks for the theater.
  • Producing events, academic lectures, symposiums, and movement workshops for the local community.

    • Information on the organization can be found below:
    https://www-stage.aac.pref.aichi.jp/index.html
    Duke Arts
    2023-2024 / Duke Arts Fellow, Durham USA
    Duke Arts is a professional presenting entity on campus that presents world-class performances, foster academic collaborations in art, and support the creation of new productions. As a Duke Arts Fellow, I worked closely with the Community Engagement team to bridge the academic community with the artist in residence and to bridge the local community with the artistic landscape at Duke.

    My role as a fellow included:
  • Producing academic lectures, workshops, and performances with the artists in residence.
  • Working with artists as thought partners to support their production of Duke Arts commissioned works.
  • Organizing events for the student community that aimed to make the arts more accessible and recognized on campus.
  • Supporting to coordinate free performance opportunities for local K-12 schools.
  • Planning, budgeting, and executing an experimental engagement initiative called "Meshroom." Facilitated cross-institutional collaborations (Duke University - University of North Carolina Greensboro) through this experimental initiative.

    • Major collaborations include:
  • Marika Niko "Meshroom" (USA, 2023/2024)
  • Michael Kliën "11th Organ I" / "11th Organ II" (USA, 2023/2024)

    • Information on the organization can be found below:
    https://dukearts.org/
